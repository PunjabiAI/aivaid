Health Tips

  • Try to indulge in physical activities as much as you can. Try to do small chores like dusting, brooming, gardening , ironing clothes and other similar stuff.
  • Regular physical activity is important for the healthy growth and development of children and young people.
  • Limit TV time. Get involved and organise outdoor activities like playing with kids, cycling, swimming etc.
  • Sit upright in your chair, with your feet touching the floor when you're working at your desk.
  • Use stairs instead of elevators to reach your work place.
  • Keep nuts, sprouts, fruit or other healthy stuff in the cupboard of your desk.
  • Take a small walk after every 2-3 hours of sitting.
  • Try getting off the bus one stop early on the way home from work, and walking.
  • People who regularly eat breakfast are less likely to be overweight, so don't skip breakfast.
  • Eat right and light to get health.
  • Fruits such as strawberries, orange, guava and pineapple have large amounts of Vitamin C and are beneficial against stress.
  • For good health, we need more than 40 different nutrients, and no single food can supply all these.
  • Don't read while walking.
  • Get up early in the morning, to remain away from health warning.
  • Eat what your body need, not you.
  • Eat fewer snacks and select healthier alternatives
  • Always hold the handrail when using stairs.
  • Meditation quietens your mind and calms your soul.You will get inner peace.
  • This will increase your Self-Awareness and Spiritual Connection.
  • When you don’t rest well, you compensate by eating more — usually junk food. So get enough rest to avoid this.
  • Also, enough sleep avoids premature aging.
  • Water is the best way to quench your thirst – and it doesn’t come with the added sugar found in fruit juices, soft drinks and other drinks.
