Diseases and Causes

A disease is an abnormal condition of an organism that impairs bodily functions, associated with specific symptoms and signs.

Breast Cancer

Breast cancer is one of the leading cause of cancer deaths among women. Breast cancer is a disease that occurs when the cell in the breast tissue change and keep on reproducing. These abnormal cells create a cluster together and form a tumor.

Cardiac arrest

Cardiac arrest is an unexpected and sudden loss of heart function, consciousness and breathing. Cardiac arrest is a caused due to the sudden electric disturbance in the heart that can disrupt the pumping action of the heart.

Headache

Headache is common and not considered as a serious illness. Even if a headache does not have any serious cause, they can disrupt the daily routine. A headache is a common condition which causes pain and discomfort in the head.

Hepatitis

THepatitis refers to the inflammatory condition of the liver. It is commonly caused due to a viral infection. But there are several other causes of hepatitis that includes autoimmune hepatitis and hepatitis that occurs as a result of the medications.

High Fever

Fever is when a humanâ€™s body temperature climbs above the normal range of 36-37 degree Centigrade or 98-100 degree in Fahrenheit. Other terms for fever include pyrexia and controlled hyperthermia.

Jaundice

Jaundice is a disease which causes yellow discoloration of the skin and whites of the eyes. This is caused due to the increased amount of bilirubin in the blood. Bilirubin is a by-product of destruction of white blood cells in the body.
